# CTR (Click-Through Rate)

CTR (Click-Through Rate) measures the percentage of ad impressions that result in clicks, indicating how engaging and relevant ads are to shoppers. Higher CTR suggests ads resonate with the target audience and are effectively capturing attention.

# Details

## Definition and Calculation

CTR is the ratio of clicks to impressions, expressed as a percentage. The formula is:

```
CTR = (clicks ÷ impressions) × 100
```

This metric shows what proportion of people who see an ad actually click on it, helping advertisers understand ad performance and audience engagement.

## Performance Context

CTR is used alongside other metrics like [ACOS](/metrics/acos.md), [CPC](/metrics/cpc.md), and conversion rate to evaluate overall campaign performance. A high CTR indicates ads are compelling and well-targeted, while a low CTR may suggest the need to optimize ad creatives, targeting, or product positioning.

### Video Performance Impact

Sponsored Products campaigns with video creatives see improved engagement compared to image-only campaigns. According to Amazon internal data from August-October 2025 in the US, Sponsored Products campaigns with video saw an average 9% uplift in CTR compared to campaigns without video.
