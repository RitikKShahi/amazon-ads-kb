# CPM (Cost-Per-Thousand Impressions)

CPM (Cost-Per-Thousand Impressions) is the average cost to deliver 1,000 impressions of advertisements. Unlike [CPC](/metrics/cpc.md) (Cost-Per-Click) where advertisers pay for clicks, CPM pricing means advertisers pay for ad display regardless of whether users click.

# Details

## Definition and Calculation

CPM represents the cost incurred per thousand ad impressions, commonly used for brand awareness and display advertising campaigns. The "M" in CPM comes from the Roman numeral for 1,000.

This metric helps advertisers understand the cost efficiency of their ad delivery and compare costs across different campaigns, placements, or publishers.

## Usage Context

CPM is typically used for campaigns focused on:

- Brand awareness and visibility
- Display and video advertising
- Situations where impression count is more important than direct clicks

While many Amazon Ads formats use [CPC](/metrics/cpc.md) pricing, CPM may be used for certain display or awareness-focused campaigns where the goal is maximizing visibility rather than direct response.
