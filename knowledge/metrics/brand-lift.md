# Brand Lift

Brand lift measures the direct impact that advertisements have on shopper perception and behaviors toward a brand. Unlike performance metrics that measure immediate clicks or conversions, brand lift quantifies changes in consumer attitudes and intent resulting from advertising exposure.

# Details

## Definition

Brand lift is calculated using post-exposure success metrics that measure changes in consumer attitudes and behaviors, including:

- **Brand awareness**: Increased recognition or recall of the brand
- **Brand consideration**: Greater likelihood to consider the brand for future purchases
- **Purchase intent**: Higher likelihood to purchase the brand's products
- **Brand perception**: Improved brand image or association

## Measurement Approach

Brand lift is measured by comparing metrics between exposed groups (people who saw ads) and control groups (similar people who didn't see ads). The difference in attitudes or behaviors between these groups represents the "lift" attributable to advertising.

This measurement approach helps advertisers understand the long-term brand-building impact of their campaigns beyond immediate response metrics like [CTR](/metrics/ctr.md) or [conversions](/metrics/conversions.md).

## Usage

Brand lift is particularly important for:

- **Brand awareness campaigns**: Measuring impact on brand recognition
- **New product launches**: Understanding introduction effectiveness
- **Share of voice**: Comparing brand impact against competitors
- **Upper-funnel optimization**: Evaluating impact before purchase decisions
