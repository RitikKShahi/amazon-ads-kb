# New-to-Brand Metrics

New-to-brand metrics describe customers who are purchasing a particular brand or product for the first time on Amazon over the past year. These metrics help advertisers understand their customer acquisition effectiveness and estimate the cost of engaging new customers on Amazon's platform.

# Details

## Definition

New-to-brand metrics identify and quantify shoppers who are making their first-ever purchase of a brand's products on Amazon. The measurement period covers the past 12 months, meaning any customer who hasn't purchased from that brand within the last year is considered "new-to-brand" when they make a purchase.

## Business Value

These metrics are particularly valuable for:

- **Customer acquisition cost**: Understanding how much it costs to acquire new customers
- **Channel efficiency**: Identifying the most efficient channels and tactics to achieve campaign goals
- **Growth measurement**: Tracking how effectively campaigns expand customer base
- **Budget optimization**: Allocating spend toward channels that best acquire new customers

## Usage in Campaign Strategy

New-to-brand metrics help advertisers:

- Estimate the cost of engaging new customers on Amazon
- Compare effectiveness of different advertising formats and placements for customer acquisition
- Make informed decisions about budget allocation between acquisition and retention
- Evaluate whether campaigns are successfully expanding brand reach beyond existing customers

This focus on new customers complements other performance metrics like [ACOS](/metrics/acos.md) and [ROAS](/metrics/roas.md) to provide a more complete picture of campaign effectiveness.
