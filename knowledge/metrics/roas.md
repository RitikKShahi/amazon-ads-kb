# ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)

ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) measures how many dollars you earn for every dollar you spend on advertising. It's the inverse of [ACOS](/metrics/acos.md), providing a direct view of advertising efficiency in terms of revenue generated per unit of spend.

# Details

## Definition and Calculation

ROAS indicates the revenue return from advertising investments. The formula is:

```
ROAS = ad revenue ÷ ad spend
```

This is calculated as total ad sales divided by total ad spend, resulting in a ratio rather than a percentage.

## Relationship to ACOS

ROAS is the mathematical inverse of [ACOS](/metrics/acos.md):

- ACOS = (ad spend ÷ ad revenue) × 100 — percentage of revenue spent on ads
- ROAS = ad revenue ÷ ad spend — revenue earned per dollar of ad spend

For example, if ACOS is 20%, ROAS would be 5.0 (5:1 return ratio). Both metrics measure the same relationship from different perspectives — cost vs. return.

## Usage

ROAS helps advertisers evaluate the direct revenue impact of their advertising spend, making it easier to understand the efficiency of campaigns in terms of return. A higher ROAS indicates better advertising efficiency, as more revenue is generated per dollar of spend.
