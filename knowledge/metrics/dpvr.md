# DPVR (Detail Page View Rate)

DPVR (Detail Page View Rate) is an Amazon-proprietary metric that measures the rate at which users view product detail pages after seeing or engaging with advertisements. It is primarily used in Amazon DSP campaign reporting to assess how effectively advertising drives traffic to product detail pages.

# Details

## Definition

DPVR is a conversion-focused metric that indicates the percentage of users who viewed a product's detail page after being exposed to or interacting with an advertisement. It helps advertisers understand how effectively their campaigns are driving consideration and purchase intent by measuring product detail page engagement.

## Usage in Amazon DSP

DPVR is one of several **Amazon-proprietary metrics** available in Amazon DSP campaign reporting, alongside:
- **ATL** (Add-to-List): Measures cart or wishlist additions
- **NTB** (New-to-Brand): Identifies first-time brand purchasers

These proprietary metrics complement standard advertising metrics to provide deeper insight into shopping behavior and campaign performance.

## Campaign Measurement

DPVR is used to:

- **Assess consideration phase effectiveness**: Measures how well ads drive product page visits
- **Evaluate creative performance**: Compare DPVR across different creative executions
- **Optimize targeting**: Identify audience segments with higher detail page engagement
- **Benchmark campaigns**: Compare DPVR performance across campaigns and time periods

## Relationship to Other Metrics

DPVR works alongside standard metrics to provide a complete view of campaign performance:

- **Impressions**: Total ad exposures
- **Clicks**: Direct ad engagement
- **DPVR**: Product detail page views (consideration signal)
- **Conversions**: Final purchase actions
- **ATL**: Shopping cart/wishlist additions (intent signal)
- **NTB**: New brand customer acquisition

## Strategic Importance

DPVR is particularly valuable for:

- **Brand awareness campaigns**: Measuring middle-of-funnel engagement
- **Product launch campaigns**: Assessing interest in new products
- **Competitive targeting**: Measuring conquest campaign effectiveness
- **Audience testing**: Comparing detail page engagement across segments
