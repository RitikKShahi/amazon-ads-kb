# ROI (Return on Investment)

ROI (Return on Investment) measures how much net profit you earn for every dollar you spend. Unlike [ROAS](/metrics/roas.md), which measures revenue return, ROI accounts for actual costs and profit margins, providing a clearer view of true advertising profitability.

# Details

## Definition and Calculation

ROI measures the profitability of advertising investments by comparing net profit to cost. The formula is:

```
ROI = (revenue - cost) ÷ cost
```

This calculation accounts for the total cost of goods sold and other expenses, not just the advertising spend, making it a more comprehensive measure of true profitability than revenue-based metrics.

## Relationship to Other Metrics

ROI differs from [ROAS](/metrics/roas.md) and [ACOS](/metrics/acos.md) in important ways:

- **ROI**: Measures profit return after all costs (revenue minus cost, divided by cost)
- **ROAS**: Measures revenue return per dollar of ad spend (revenue divided by ad spend)
- **ACOS**: Measures ad spend as percentage of revenue (ad spend divided by revenue)

ROI is particularly useful for understanding the true bottom-line impact of advertising campaigns, as it considers the actual profitability rather than just top-line revenue or spend efficiency.
