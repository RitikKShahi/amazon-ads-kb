# Reach

Reach measures the total number of unique users who are shown the same advertisement across any webpage or screen. Unlike [impressions](/metrics/impressions.md), which count total ad displays, reach counts individual people reached, providing insight into audience size and campaign breadth.

# Details

## Definition

Reach represents the number of distinct individuals who have seen an advertisement, regardless of how many times the ad was shown to each person. This makes reach a key metric for understanding:

- **Audience size**: How many unique people are being exposed to the campaign
- **Campaign breadth**: The spread of advertising across the audience
- **Brand awareness**: Potential impact on total addressable audience

## Relationship to Impressions

While [impressions](/metrics/impressions.md) count every ad display (including multiple views by the same person), reach counts unique individuals. This distinction is important for understanding:

- **Frequency**: The average number of times each person sees an ad (impressions ÷ reach)
- **Campaign efficiency**: Whether budget is reaching new people or repeatedly showing to the same users
- **Saturation**: How much of the target audience has been exposed to the messaging

High impressions with low reach indicates the same users are seeing ads frequently, while high reach suggests broader audience exposure.
