# TACOS (Total Advertising Cost of Sale)

TACOS (Total Advertising Cost of Sale) measures advertising spend compared to a seller's total sales revenue, including both ad-attributed sales and organic sales. Unlike [ACOS](/metrics/acos.md), which only considers ad revenue, TACOS provides a holistic view of all sales in relation to advertising spend, acknowledging the symbiotic relationship between paid and organic sales.

# Details

## Definition and Calculation

TACOS calculates the total advertising spend required to generate one total unit of revenue. The formula is:

```
TACoS = Total Advertising Cost / Total Revenue
```

This can also be expressed as:

```
TACoS = (Advertising Spend ÷ Total Revenue) × 100
```

Where Total Revenue is the combined total of ad-attributed sales and organic sales.

## Difference from ACOS

[ACOS](/metrics/acos.md) (Advertising Cost of Sale) is calculated by dividing ad spend by ad revenue only, whereas TACOS divides ad spend by total revenue (both ad-attributed and organic sales). This makes TACOS more important for overall PPC strategy than ACOS alone because PPC advertising can help index products for relevant keywords, which improves organic ranking.

**Example**: If $100 ad spend generates $100 in PPC sales and $900 in organic sales:
- ACOS = 100% ($100 ÷ $100)
- TACOS = 10% ($100 ÷ $1,000)

This illustrates how TACOS captures the broader impact of advertising on overall business performance.

## Strategic Importance

TACOS helps acknowledge the symbiotic relationship between paid sales and organic sales that [ACOS](/metrics/acos.md) does not explicitly capture. A positive sales cycle occurs when increased ad spend leads to greater ad sales, which improves organic ranking and sales. Conversely, a negative sales cycle occurs when decreased ad spend leads to lower ad sales, resulting in reduced organic rankings and sales.

Amazon boosts product listings based on sales volume, creating a snowball effect where PPC ads increase visibility, leading to more searches and organic sales.

## What is a Good TACOS?

A good TACOS is indicated by a decreasing or flat TACOS percentage over time rather than a specific numerical target. Lower TACOS values indicate an optimized advertising strategy where less ad spend generates each sale.
