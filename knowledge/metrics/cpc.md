# CPC (Cost-Per-Click)

CPC (Cost-Per-Click) is the average cost paid each time someone clicks an advertisement. This is the core pricing model for [Sponsored Products](/ad-products/sponsored-products.md) and other Amazon Ads formats, meaning advertisers only pay when shoppers engage with their ads rather than for impressions.

# Details

## Definition and Calculation

CPC represents the actual cost incurred per ad click, calculated by dividing the total amount spent on an ad by the number of clicks it received:

```
CPC = amount spent ÷ clicks
```

This metric helps advertisers understand their actual cost per customer interaction and monitor bidding efficiency over time.

## Pay-Per-Click Model

Amazon's advertising model is fundamentally pay-per-click (PPC), meaning:

- Advertisers pay only when a shopper clicks an ad
- There are no monthly or upfront fees for [Sponsored Products](/ad-products/sponsored-products.md) campaigns
- Costs are incurred based on actual shopper engagement rather than ad display
- Advertisers can control daily budgets and maximum bids to manage spend

## Bidding System

Advertisers use a bidding system where they bid the maximum amount they're willing to pay when a shopper clicks an ad. More competitive bids increase the likelihood that ads will be displayed when they match a customer's shopping query. The actual CPC paid may be less than the maximum bid depending on competition and auction dynamics.

### Bid Strategy Considerations

Effective bid management involves:

- **Maximum bid setting**: Establish the highest amount willing to pay per click
- **Competitive analysis**: Research typical bids for target keywords in your category
- **Performance-based bidding**: Adjust bids based on keyword conversion rates
- **Portfolio management**: Apply different bid strategies across keyword segments
- **Dynamic bidding**: Use automated bidding strategies to optimize for campaigns

## CPC Pricing Across Ad Formats

### Sponsored Products CPC
- **Standard pricing**: Pay only when customers click on product ads
- **Keyword-based bidding**: Different CPC for different keyword targets
- **Category variation**: Competitive categories have higher average CPC
- **Quality score impact**: Better ad relevance can lower effective CPC

### Video Ads CPC
Video ads maintain the same CPC pricing model while offering enhanced engagement:

- **Same pay-per-click structure**: Advertisers pay only when viewers engage with video content
- **No premium for video format**: Video ads don't cost more per click than image ads
- **Enhanced engagement potential**: Video format provides 9% higher [CTR](/metrics/ctr.md) at similar CPC
- **Bid boosting available**: 0-900% bid boost range to increase video ad visibility

### Bid Boosting for Video
Video ads support bid adjustment capabilities for enhanced control:

- **0-900% bid boost range**: Advertisers can increase video ad bids up to 9x default
- **Performance-based allocation**: Higher bids for better-performing video creative
- **Placement priority**: Increased bids improve video ad placement and visibility
- **Budget control**: Bid boosting allows strategic allocation of budget to top performers

## CPC Benchmarks and Variation

CPC varies significantly based on product category, competition level, and targeting precision:

### Industry CPC Ranges
- **Competitive categories**: $1.50-3.50+ per click (electronics, home improvement)
- **Moderate competition**: $0.75-1.50 per click (apparel, home goods)
- **Lower competition**: $0.30-0.75 per click (niche products, specialty items)
- **Long-tail keywords**: Often $0.15-0.50 per click due to lower competition

### CPC Optimization Strategies
- **Long-tail targeting**: Focus on specific, less competitive keywords
- **Match type strategy**: Use EXACT match for high-CPC keywords to improve relevance
- **Quality improvement**: Better ad performance can reduce effective CPC over time
- **Portfolio bidding**: Apply different bid strategies based on keyword performance segments

## Cost Management and Efficiency

Effective CPC management requires balancing cost with performance:

### Performance Metrics Integration
CPC should be analyzed alongside:
- **[ACOS](/metrics/acos.md)**: Ensure click costs don't exceed profit margins
- **[CTR](/metrics/ctr.md)**: Higher CTR can improve ad quality and reduce CPC
- **Conversion rate**: Strong conversion rates justify higher CPC investment
- **Average order value**: Higher order values can support more aggressive bidding

### Budget Optimization
Strategic CPC management involves:
- **Daily budget setting**: Establish maximum spend limits per campaign
- **Bid caps**: Set maximum CPC limits to prevent cost escalation
- **Portfolio management**: Allocate budget across campaigns based on performance
- **Seasonal adjustment**: Adjust bids based on seasonal demand and competition
