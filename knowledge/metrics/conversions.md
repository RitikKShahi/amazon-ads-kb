# Conversions

Conversions are actions that customers complete after engaging with advertisements, such as clicking Add to Cart or completing a purchase. This metric measures the direct business results generated by advertising campaigns, indicating how effectively ads drive desired customer behaviors.

# Details

## Definition

A conversion represents a completed customer action that contributes to business objectives. Common conversion types in Amazon advertising include:

- **Add to Cart**: Customer adds product to shopping cart
- **Purchase**: Customer completes a purchase transaction
- **Product Page Views**: Customer views product detail pages
- **Other engagement actions**: Depending on campaign goals

## Performance Measurement

Conversions are critical for evaluating campaign performance alongside other metrics:

- **Conversion Rate (CVR)**: Percentage of clicks that result in conversions
- **[ACOS](/metrics/acos.md)**: Ad spend efficiency relative to attributed sales
- **[CTR](/metrics/ctr.md)**: Click-through rate from impressions to clicks
- **Cost Per Conversion**: Average ad spend to acquire one conversion

High conversion rates indicate effective targeting, compelling product offerings, and good alignment between ads and customer intent. Low conversion rates may suggest optimization opportunities in product listings, pricing, or ad targeting.
