# Sponsored Products

Sponsored Products are cost-per-click ([CPC](/metrics/cpc.md)) advertisements that promote individual product listings on Amazon's platforms. Ads appear in shopping results, on product pages, and beyond Amazon on other sites, helping products reach more shoppers when they're looking for what to buy.

# Details

## Overview

Sponsored Products campaigns use a pay-per-click model where advertisers pay only when shoppers click their ads — there are no monthly or upfront fees. Campaigns can be created using either image or video creatives, with video campaigns showing an average 9% uplift in [CTR](/metrics/ctr.md) compared to image-only campaigns according to Amazon internal data (August-October 2025, US).

## Ad Placement

Sponsored Products ads appear in multiple locations:

**On Amazon**:
- At the top of shopping results
- Alongside shopping results
- Within shopping results
- On product detail pages

**Beyond Amazon**:
- Other Amazon-owned and operated sites
- Third-party destinations and websites

Ads support browser and app experiences across desktop, mobile, and tablet devices. When a Sponsored Products ad is clicked, customers are directed to the advertised product's detail page.

## Campaign Management

### Bidding System

Sponsored Products uses a bidding system where:
- Advertisers bid the maximum amount they're willing to pay when a shopper clicks an ad
- More competitive bids increase the likelihood that ads will be displayed when they match a customer's shopping query
- Actual cost per click may be less than the maximum bid

### Budget Management

Sponsored Products campaigns use a daily budget system:
- Advertisers set the maximum amount they're willing to spend per day over a calendar month
- Daily budgets are not paced throughout the day — a budget could be spent in minutes if there's high shopper interest
- Total campaign cost never exceeds the daily budget multiplied by the number of days in the campaign period
- Budgets can be increased or decreased while ads are running

### Targeting Options

Advertisers can either:
- **Manually select keywords** to target specific search terms
- **Let Amazon's systems automatically target** relevant keywords using machine learning

Sponsored Products support flexible keyword and product targeting options, allowing precise control over which searches and products trigger ads.

## Eligibility Requirements

### Who Can Advertise

Sponsored Products are available to:
- Professional sellers
- Vendors
- Book vendors
- Kindle Direct Publishing (KDP) authors
- Agencies

### Product Eligibility

Products must:
- Be in one or more eligible categories
- Be eligible for the Featured Offer (Buy Box)
- Not be adult products, used products, refurbished products, or in closed categories

**Important**: Sponsored Products ads only appear when the advertised items are in stock.

## Campaign Creation

Creating a Sponsored Products campaign involves six steps:
1. Register for sponsored ads
2. Sign in and choose Sponsored Products
3. Add products to advertise
4. Define targeting and bidding strategies
5. Choose campaign settings (budget, duration, etc.)
6. Launch the campaign

## Performance Measurement

Sponsored Products advertisers can generate reports that provide insights on:
- Sales and performance of advertised products or targets
- How different ad placements are performing
- Key metrics including [ACOS](/metrics/acos.md), [CTR](/metrics/ctr.md), [CPC](/metrics/cpc.md), and [conversions](/metrics/conversions.md)

## New Seller Promotion

New sellers on the Professional selling plan can earn up to $1,000 in ad credits when they:
- Launch and spend on Sponsored Products within 30 days of launching their first campaign
- Have listed their first buyable ASIN in an eligible marketplace within the last 90 days
